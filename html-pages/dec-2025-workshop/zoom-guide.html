Test your camera and microphone BEFORE the workshop so you're not scrambling on the day!

1

The easiest way to test is to use Zoom's free test meeting. Click the button below:

Test Your Zoom Setup Now

This will start a test meeting where you can see and hear yourself. It's completely private ‚Äî only you can see it!

2

Test Your Speaker (Can You Hear?)

Step 1: Open Zoom on your computer.

Step 2: Click the gear icon ‚öôÔ∏è (Settings) in the top right.

Step 3: Click "Audio" in the left menu.

Step 4: Click "Test Speaker".

Step 5: You should hear a ringtone. If you hear it, your speaker works!

üîä Don't Hear Anything?

Make sure your computer volume is turned up (not muted). Check that the correct speaker is selected in the dropdown menu.

3

Step 1: In the same Audio settings screen...

Step 2: Click "Test Mic".

Step 3: Speak into your microphone: "Testing, 1, 2, 3..."

Step 4: You should hear your voice played back to you. If you hear yourself, your microphone works!

Step 1: In Zoom Settings, click "Video" in the left menu.

Step 2: You should see yourself in the preview window!

Step 3: If you see yourself, your camera is working.

üìπ Don't See Yourself?

üåü Pro Tip: Good Lighting Makes a Big Difference!

Sit facing a window or have a lamp in front of you (not behind you). This lights up your face so others can see you clearly. If a bright window is behind you, you'll look like a dark shadow!

Basic Zoom Controls

Once you're in the meeting, here are the important buttons you'll use.

üé§

Mute / Unmute (Your Microphone)

In the bottom left corner of your screen, you'll see a microphone icon.

‚Ä¢ Microphone with a red line through it = You are MUTED (others can't hear you)

‚Ä¢ Microphone without a line = You are UNMUTED (others can hear you)

üîá Important

During the workshop, please stay muted unless you're asked to speak. This prevents background noise from disrupting the presentation.

üìπ

Video On / Off (Your Camera)

Next to the microphone, you'll see a camera icon.

‚Ä¢ Camera with a red line through it = Your video is OFF (others can't see you)

‚Ä¢ Camera without a line = Your video is ON (others can see you)

Click the camera icon to turn your video on or off.

üí° Tip

We encourage you to turn your video ON so we can see your smiling face! But it's okay to leave it off if you prefer.

Step 2: Type your message in the box at the bottom.

Step 3: Press Enter on your keyboard (or tap Send) to send it.

The chat is a great way to ask questions or share your thoughts without interrupting!

Zoom lets you choose how you see the other participants. Here are your options.

üé§

When to use it: During the workshop presentations! This view helps you focus on Wallace Hamilton and Mark Hamilton while they speak.

üëç We Recommend This View

Speaker View is best for watching presentations because it automatically shows you whoever is speaking.

üí° Tip
