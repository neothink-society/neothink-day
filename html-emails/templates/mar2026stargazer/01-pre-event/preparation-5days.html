LEVEL 3 • STARGAZER IMMERSION • 5 DAYS

5 Days.

Your environment is part of the immersion.

What happens on March 27–29 happens in two places simultaneously: inside you, and in the space around you. The Hamilton family has designed this immersion assuming you have protected both.

Your environment on those three days is not a backdrop. It's a variable. The people around you, the noise level, the notifications firing, the interruptions—each one costs something. Five days is enough time to eliminate every one of them.

 

Set Up Your Space This Week

Claim a dedicated space.
A private room where you will not be interrupted for a full day. Not a coffee shop. Not a shared room. A space that belongs to you completely for Friday, Saturday, and Sunday.

Prepare your food in advance.
Lunch breaks are short. Have meals and snacks ready so you stay in the room—and stay in the state. Leaving to figure out food mid-day is a disruption that takes longer to recover from than it appears.

Silence everything.
Phone on Do Not Disturb. Notifications off. Let the people in your life know you are unavailable. Not partially available—unavailable. The world will still be there Monday morning.

Camera on. No exceptions.
The Hamilton family operates as if everyone is physically present. Camera off is the equivalent of leaving the room. You are in this immersion. Be visible in it.

Why This Matters More Than It Sounds

The breakthroughs that happen in this immersion happen in accumulated depth—not in individual moments. They require sustained, uninterrupted presence over hours, not minutes.

Every time you look away—at a phone, at a notification, at someone walking in—the depth resets. You're not just losing a moment. You're losing the accumulation. The environment you build this week determines what's possible next week.
