## Call-to-Action (CTA) Strategy

### Primary CTA Rules
1. **One per email** - Maximum focus, clear action
2. **Above the fold** - Visible without scrolling (mobile especially)
3. **Repeated strategically** - Once at top, once at bottom maximum
4. **Action-oriented** - Start with verbs: Watch, Join, Get, Unlock, Access
5. **Specific** - Say exactly what happens: "Watch Now" not "Click Here"

## Email Type Templates

### 1. Announcement Email

**Purpose**: Introduce event, opportunity, or major news

**Structure**:
1. Classification header
2. Badge (if applicable)
3. Headline: What this is
4. Subheading: Why it matters
5. Key details (date, time, format)
6. What to expect (3-5 bullets)
7. Primary CTA
8. Additional context
9. Footer

**Messaging Focus**:
- Excitement and exclusivity
- Clear value proposition
- Specific logistics
- What makes this different

**Example Subject**: "You're In: Day of Enlightenment Workshop"

### 2. Countdown/Preparation Email

**Purpose**: Build anticipation, provide logistics, ensure readiness

**Structure**:
1. Urgency classification ([X] days out)
2. Countdown visual or headline
3. Key action items (numbered or bulleted)
4. What to prepare (checklist format)
5. Primary CTA (access details)
6. Reminder of what's coming
7. Footer

**Messaging Focus**:
- Preparation builds results
- Everything they need to know
- Build anticipation and readiness
- Remove friction and anxiety

**Example Subject**: "48 Hours: Everything You Need"

### 3. Day-Of/Urgent Email

**Purpose**: Drive immediate action, provide access, maximize attendance

**Structure**:
1. URGENT header (emoji + caps)
2. Large headline: "TODAY" or "NOW"
3. Primary CTA (massive, impossible to miss)
4. Brief context (1-2 sentences max)
5. Time details if applicable
6. That's it - no distractions

**Messaging Focus**:
- Extreme brevity
- Maximum urgency
- Zero friction
- One action only

**Example Subject**: "üî¥ LIVE NOW: Join Immediately"

8. Footer

**Messaging Focus**:

- Breakthrough, rewire, transform, disintegrate, architect, build, create

## A/B Testing Guidelines

### What to Test

**Subject Lines**:
- Emoji vs no emoji
- Question vs statement
- Short vs medium length
- Urgency vs curiosity

**CTAs**:
- Button text variations
- Button color (gold standard, test against white for some audiences)
- Button placement (one vs two locations)
- Button size

**Content Length**:
- Minimal (day-of emails) vs comprehensive (post-event)
- Bullets vs paragraphs
- With testimonials vs without

**Sending Time**:
- Morning vs afternoon vs evening
- Weekday vs weekend
- Time before event (optimal countdown timing)

### What NOT to Test

- Brand fonts (use correct ones always)
- Brand colors (gold, black, red for urgency)
- Support email
- Brand name ("The Neothink Society")
- Mobile responsiveness (always required)

### Testing Process

1. **Hypothesis**: "I believe [changing X] will improve [metric Y] because [reason]"
2. **Split**: 50/50 or 70/30 (winner/control)
3. **Measure**: Open rate, click rate, conversion rate
4. **Duration**: Send to statistically significant sample
5. **Decide**: Implement winner for future emails
6. **Document**: Record results for reference
